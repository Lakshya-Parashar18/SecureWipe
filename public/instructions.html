SecureWipe Instructions

Complete guide to securely erasing your drives and protecting your data

üìã Preparation

  1. Backup Important Data: Before proceeding, ensure you have backed up any important data you want to keep. SecureWipe will permanently destroy all data on the selected drives.
  2. Gather Required Materials:
    • USB drive (8GB or larger)
    • Computer with internet connection
    • Target computer with drives to wipe
  3. Download Required Tools: You'll need either Rufus (Windows) or Etcher (Mac/Linux) to create the bootable USB.

üñ¥ Create Bootable USB

  1. Insert USB Drive: Connect your USB drive to your computer. Note: All data on the USB will be erased.
  2. Launch USB Tool: Open Rufus (Windows) or Etcher (Mac/Linux).
  3. Select ISO File: Choose the SecureWipe ISO file you downloaded.
  4. Select USB Drive: Choose your USB drive from the list.
  5. Start Flashing: Click "Start" (Rufus) or "Flash!" (Etcher) and wait for completion.

üí° Pro Tip

For Rufus users: Use the default settings (MBR partition scheme, FAT32 file system). For Etcher users: The tool will automatically configure the USB correctly.

üöÄ Boot from USB

  1. Insert USB: Plug the bootable USB into the computer with drives you want to wipe.
  2. Access Boot Menu: Restart the computer and press the boot menu key during startup:
    • Dell: F12
    • HP: F9 or Esc
    • Lenovo: F12 or F1
    • ASUS: F8 or Esc
    • Mac: Hold Option key during startup
  3. Select USB: Choose your USB drive from the boot menu.
  4. Wait for Boot: SecureWipe will load automatically. This may take a few minutes.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important

Make sure to select the correct USB drive. Booting from the wrong drive could cause issues with your system.

üßπ Wipe Your Drives

  1. Launch SecureWipe: Once booted, the SecureWipe interface will appear automatically.
  2. Select Drives: Carefully select the drives you want to wipe. Double-check to avoid wiping the wrong drives.
  3. Choose Wipe Method: Select your preferred wiping algorithm:
    • Quick Wipe: Single pass, faster (for non-sensitive data)
    • Secure Wipe: Multiple passes, more thorough (recommended)
    • Military Grade: Maximum security, slower (for highly sensitive data)
  4. Confirm Action: Review your selections and confirm to start the wiping process.
  5. Wait for Completion: The process may take several hours depending on drive size and method chosen.
  6. Generate Certificate: After completion, SecureWipe will generate a certificate proving the wipe was successful.

üìä Process Times

Approximate times for a 1TB drive: Quick Wipe (30 minutes), Secure Wipe (2-3 hours), Military Grade (6-8 hours). Times vary based on drive speed and computer performance.

üìú Certificate & Verification

  1. Save Certificate: The wipe certificate will be saved to your USB drive. This proves the wipe was completed successfully.
  2. Verify Certificate: Use our online verification tool to validate the certificate's authenticity.
  3. Store Safely: Keep the certificate in a safe place as proof of secure data destruction.
  4. Recycle Responsibly: Now you can safely recycle or resell your device without worrying about data recovery.

üîß Troubleshooting

  • USB won't boot: Try recreating the USB with different settings or use a different USB drive.
  • Boot menu not appearing: Check BIOS settings to enable USB boot or try different boot keys.
  • SecureWipe won't start: Ensure your computer supports UEFI/Legacy boot and try different boot modes.
  • Drive not detected: Check drive connections and ensure drives are properly powered.
  • Wipe process stuck: Wait longer (large drives take time) or restart and try a different wipe method.
  • Certificate not generated: Ensure USB has enough free space and try the process again.
